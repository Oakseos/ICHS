ICHS's History

Iworoko Community High School was established on 9th September 1979. The pioneer Principal was Chief M.O.Kayode. In August 2005, like all other public secondary schools in the in the state, the school was split into two Junior and Senior Schools according to the government policy.

But in September 2011, the two schools were brought together again as a result of he rearticulation of public secondary schools in the state. The school is currently being headed by Mrs. G.O. Familoni.

Our Goal

To give the boys and girls qualitative education with emphasis on both morals and academics, so that they will grow to contribute meaningfully to the development of man and make the world around them better than they met it.

Serene Environment

We are located in an easily accessible and serene environment within the Government Reservation Area (GRA), Alagbaka, Akure .

Key Of Success

Our staff (academic and non-academic) are the best. They make learning easy and interesting for our students..

Our Curriculum

In line with the national policy of education the school operates a wide curriculum to take care of all students. The scope is large enough to give each student a broad-based education to enable them make a career choice consistent with his/her aptitudes and interest

The Students Representative Council

The students representative Council comprises of

  • The School Prefects
  • All class captains
  • The Vice Principal
  • 3 teachers
The S.R.C. Is a liaison body between the school authority and the students. It meets occasionally to discus issues concerning the progress of the school in general. Apart from participating in the physical and material development of the School, the S.R.C. reduces area of friction between the administration and the students. The senior Boy is the Chairman while the Senior Girl is the Vice-chairman. One of the prefects is appointed as secretary to take the minutes while the Vice-principal co-ordinates the activities.
