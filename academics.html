Our Curriculum

In line with the national policy of education the school operates a wide curriculum to take care of all students. The scope is large enough to give each student a broad-based education to enable them make a career choice consistent with his/her aptitudes and interest

SSS Curriculum

The following subject are offered at J.S.S

English Language, Mathematics, Basic science, Home-economics, Computer education, Business studies, Basic Technology, French, Visual Art, Music, Christian Religious Knowledge, Arabic Language and Physical / Health Education.

Six (6) Core Subjects offerd in S.S. Classes are

  • English Language
  • Mathematics
  • One Nigeria Language (Yoruba)
  • One of Physics / Chemistry / Biology
  • One of Geography / History / Lit-in English
  • One of Vocational subjects.

Electives subjects available to choose from include :

Agricultural Science,Arabic,Christian Religious Studies,Chemistry,Commerce,Economics,Book Keeping / Account,Literature-in English,Visual Art,French,Further Mathematics,Geography,Government,Food and Nutrition,Islamic Religious Studies,Music,Physical and Health Education,Physics,Computer Science,Technical Drawing.

he House System

The school is divided into four different Houses. Each house has separate colour representation. the houses are :

  1. Red
  2. Blue
  3. Yellow
  4. Green
The house system is used in Sports and academic competitions within the school. Each house has its own Housemasters / House mistresses who supervise House practices in Sports and other Competitions.

Games and Sports

Opportunities exist in the school for participating in games and sports, and every student is expected to enlist interest in at least one of the following Football, Basketball, Table Tennis, Athletics, Volleyball and Tenniquot.

Spirituality

Every student is expected to avail himself / herself of whatever opportunity the school offers him/her for the practice of his / her religion. Exemption can only be granted if a letter addressed to the principal has been previously received from a student’s parents or guardian asking that he / she be excused from practicing a particular religion.

Alumni

Students are encouraged to join Clubs and not cults. The societies provide necessary social interaction among the students through their meeting. The existing approved school societies are as follows: -

  • Jet Club
  • Family Life Club
  • Literacy and Debating Society
  • Geographical Society
  • Young farmers Club
  • Akomolede Yoruba
  • Commonwealth Club
  • Exam Success Club
  • Press Club
  • War Against Drug Abuse
  • Christian Fellowship
  • Life Club
Students are free to join any society of their interest. For any society wishing to embark on an external activity (inter-School activity or excursion) the approval of the principal becomes imperative. In case of an excursion. Parents have to express their consent in writing before their children can be allowed to go on the excusion. In matters of finance, societies are expected to be self-sustaining. The school however frowns at extravagant projects. The principal must be in the know of the account of each society.

Rules and Regulations

  • All students are expected to behave in a responsible manner both in and outside the school
  • Students are expected to come punctually and regularly to school. If there is any cause for them to go out they should obtain permission form appropriate quarters.
  • Students should not bring Radio Cassette players or any electronic material to the schooll
  • Student must wear correct Uniform whenever they are coming to school or representing the school anywhere. Brown sandals (not Slippers) are only authorized types of footwear. Slippers are strictly prohibited in the school premises. Medium of expression should only be in English. The uniform must not be worn with other forms of addresses. Students are not allowed to wear cardigan and pullovers than the ones approved by the school.
  • Cosmetics and any form of make- up is prohibited.
  • Girls are not allowed to wear dropping earrings to school
  • Fighting, vandalism and rowdiness are strictly prohibited.
  • Students should respond to bells immediately and react accordingly.
  • Class captains should always see to the cleanliness and good arrangement of lockers and chairs in their respective classes.
  • Lateness and absence from assemblies are not allowed.
  • Students are expected to be good ambassadors of the school at public or outside engagements.

  • The school buttery is open to students only at break times.
  • In case of any damage to the school property, the offending students will be surcharged.
  • Students are expected to stand when a member of staff arrives to start a lesson in their class.
  • No student should go to toilet during lesson change-over without the permission of the member of staff taking the next lesson.
  • During the lesson time, students should not be found outside the classroom without the knowledge and permission of the class teacher.
  • Students should report to their respective class teacher or year tutors if they fall sick during the school hours. If need be, such student would be taken to the hospital for treatment and the hospital bill would be sent to the student’s parent. As much as possible sick student should stay at home for treatment.
  • The senior student are expected to guide and direct the junior ones rather than take the advantages of their tender age to make life difficult for them in the school compound. Physical combat, beating or maltreating of the young ones constitute a very serious offence that may terminate the stay of the offender in the school.
  • Stealing is illegal; usage of another student’s property is a serious offence.
  • Students are not expected to revive visitors during the school hours without the permission of the school authorities.
  • Cleaning: - The compound must be kept tidy at all times. Pieces of papers, nylons empty tins, and any other waste materials must be disposed off properly. Students should not pass by any litter without picking it. Teachers on duty will always see to the cleanliness of the compound.
